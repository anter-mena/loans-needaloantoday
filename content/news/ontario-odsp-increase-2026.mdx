**Ontario ODSP increase 2026** rates took effect July 1, raising indexed basic-needs and shelter maximums by 1.9%. Ontario lists up to $1,436 monthly for an eligible single person receiving both maximum components, but actual payments vary. The increase is not a separate bonus, and readers should use their benefit statement—not a social-media table—to identify the household-specific change.

*Published July 18, 2026. This article is general benefits information. Eligibility and amounts depend on official ODSP decisions and individual circumstances.*

## Quick answer: indexed components rose 1.9%

The [Ontario income-support page](https://www.ontario.ca/page/income-support-odsp) says the maximum for a single person can reach $1,436, depending on the situation. Published rate tables divide that amount into up to $825 for basic needs and $611 for shelter.

The **Ontario ODSP increase 2026** is applied within the regular calculation. Rent below the shelter maximum, family composition, other income and benefit rules can produce a different payment.

## Ontario ODSP increase 2026: 7 essential facts

| Fact | Reader implication |
|---|---|
| Effective date | July 1, 2026 |
| Indexed change | 1.9% |
| Single maximum | Up to $1,436 monthly |
| Basic-needs maximum | $825 for a single person |
| Shelter maximum | $611 for a single person |
| Household rates | Differ by family composition |
| Federal CDB | Ontario says it is not deducted from ODSP |

### Maximum is not the same as automatic payment

Shelter support depends on eligible shelter costs up to a ceiling. A recipient paying less than the maximum may not receive the full shelter component. Income, living arrangement, spouse and dependants also affect the calculation.

Review the benefit statement line by line. Compare the old and new basic-needs and shelter figures, then check whether income or household information changed in the same month.

### Ontario Works did not receive the same general increase

The [Income Security Advocacy Centre](https://incomesecurity.org/ow-and-odsp-rates-and-ocb-as-of-july-2026-social-assistance-is-stuck-in-time/) rate sheet distinguishes indexed ODSP amounts from Ontario Works rates. Do not apply 1.9% to every provincial assistance payment. Ontario Child Benefit amounts and family configurations have separate figures.

## What to do if the amount looks wrong

Check MyBenefits messages and whether requested documents remain outstanding. Contact the caseworker through an official channel and ask for the calculation in writing. Note the date, name and explanation. If the decision remains disputed, review internal-review and appeal rights promptly; community legal clinics or [Steps to Justice](https://stepstojustice.ca/news/new-odsp-rates-2/) can explain the process.

Do not borrow against the maximum advertised amount before the deposit is confirmed. If an essential bill is due first, ask the provider about a hardship extension and use our [fast-money guide](/blog/how-to-get-money-fast-canada) to check benefits and lower-cost help before credit.

## Bottom line

The **Ontario ODSP increase 2026** provides a 1.9% indexed adjustment, but it does not erase the need to verify a personal statement. Confirm each component, keep the Canada Disability Benefit separate in the calculation and challenge a suspected error through the documented ODSP process.
