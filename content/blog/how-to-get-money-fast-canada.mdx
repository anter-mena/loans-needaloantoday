When an unexpected bill lands and your account is empty, the question is simple and stressful: **how to get money fast in Canada** without making next month worse. Take a breath — you have more options than it feels like right now, and several can put cash in your hands today. This guide walks through them from fastest to cheapest, with honest notes on what each one really costs, so you can pick the option that solves the problem instead of deepening it.

![How to get money fast in Canada: a hand holding a Canadian five-dollar bill beside a bag](https://images.pexels.com/photos/14046229/pexels-photo-14046229.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&w=1200)

## Quick Answer

For a small, one-time shortfall, an interest-free cash advance app (Bree, Nyble or KOHO Cover) is usually the fastest low-cost move — minutes to same-day, no traditional credit check. Need a larger amount today? A same-day e-transfer installment loan is quick but pricier, capped at 35% APR. Want to avoid borrowing entirely? Selling something you own or picking up instant-pay gig work gets you cash with no interest at all. The rule to remember: **the fastest options are often the most expensive**, so borrow only what you can comfortably repay.

## How to Get Money Fast in Canada, Ranked by Speed

Here is the honest trade-off between speed and cost. Everything near the top is quick; everything near the bottom is cheaper. Where you land depends on how much you need and how soon.

| Option | How fast | Typical cost | Credit check? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cash advance app (Bree, Nyble, KOHO Cover) | Minutes to same day | 0% interest + small membership/express fees | No — reads your bank data |
| Same-day e-transfer installment loan | Minutes to hours | Up to 35% APR (federal cap) | Often a soft check |
| Payday loan | Minutes to same day | ~$14–$17 per $100 (very high effective rate) | Usually none |

| Instant-pay gig work | Same day to 2 days | Free — you earn it | No |
| Payroll advance from your employer | Next pay run or sooner | Usually free | No |

## Cash Advance Apps: Money in Minutes, Little or No Interest

Cash advance apps have become the go-to for a small gap before payday because they are fast, forgiving on credit, and charge no interest on the advance itself. Instead of a credit check, they connect to your chequing account and read your recent deposits to decide how much you can safely borrow. That is why they can approve newcomers, students and gig workers the banks often turn down.

### Bree

Bree offers the largest advances of the popular apps — **interest-free up to about $750**. There is no interest and no mandatory membership; Bree makes its money through a small optional express-transfer fee if you want the money in minutes instead of a few business days, plus an optional tip. For a genuine one-off emergency, that can be a fair price for speed. If you want to see how it stacks up against similar apps, our guide to [Bree alternatives in Canada](/blog/bree-alternatives-canada) breaks down the field.

### Nyble

Nyble gives you an **interest-free line of up to about $250** with no mandatory membership to access the base advance. Its standout feature is that it reports to Equifax, so used responsibly it can actually help you *build* credit while covering a shortfall — a rare combination. Faster transfers and extra perks sit behind a small optional premium tier.

### KOHO Cover

KOHO Cover is an **overdraft-style coverage feature** built into a KOHO account. For a flat monthly membership you get fee-free coverage on shortfalls up to your limit — no interest and no per-use charge. It only makes sense if you already use KOHO or want to, since it lives inside their spending account rather than working as a standalone app.

The common thread: these apps advertise 0% interest, and that part is true, but they still earn money through memberships, express fees and tips. On a small, short advance those fees can add up to a steep *effective* cost, so total every charge before you tap "borrow." For a deeper look at how the bank connection works, see our explainer on [cash advance apps that use Flinks](/blog/cash-advance-apps-that-use-flinks).

## Instant e-Transfer Loans: Fast, But Watch the Cost

If you need more than an app will advance, many Canadian lenders offer same-day loans funded by [Interac e-Transfer](https://www.interac.ca/en/consumers/products/interac-e-transfer/), which lands in your account almost instantly once you are approved. Approval can take minutes, often with only a soft check or a quick look at your banking activity rather than a hard credit pull. Some lenders don't even require a manual bank login — see [loans with no bank verification in Canada](/blog/loans-with-no-bank-verification-canada) for how those work.

Speed is the upside. Cost is the catch, and there are two very different products here:

![Making a fast contactless phone payment in Canada, showing how quickly digital money can move](https://images.pexels.com/photos/6612717/pexels-photo-6612717.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&w=1200)

**Payday loans** are the classic fast option. Each province caps the maximum cost at roughly **$14 to $17 per $100 borrowed** for a term of about two weeks (for example, $15 per $100 in Ontario). That sounds small, but rolled into an annual rate it is several hundred percent — which is why payday loans are best kept for true one-time emergencies and repaid on the first due date.

**Installment loans** spread repayment over months in fixed payments. Since **January 1, 2025**, the federal criminal rate of interest cap means most installment and personal loans are limited to **35% APR** — far cheaper than a payday loan for anything you can't clear in one pay cycle. (Payday loans made under provincial rules are exempt from that federal cap, which is exactly why they cost more.)

| | Payday loan | Installment loan |
|---|---|---|
| Repayment | Lump sum, ~2 weeks | Fixed payments over months |
| Cost | ~$14–$17 per $100 | Capped at 35% APR |
| Best for | A tiny, one-time gap | A larger or spread-out need |

Whatever you choose, ask for the **total cost of borrowing in dollars**, not just the rate. ## Fast Cash Without Borrowing

The cheapest money is the money you don't have to repay. Before you take on any loan, it is worth asking whether one of these could cover the gap:

- **Sell something you already own.** A phone, console, bike or spare furniture can turn into cash the same day on Facebook Marketplace or Kijiji. Price it to move and meet safely in a public place.
- **Pick up instant-pay gig work.** Food delivery, rideshare, or task apps let you cash out earnings quickly — sometimes the same day or next. If your income is irregular, our guide to [loans for self-employed and gig workers](/resources/guides/loans-for-self-employed) covers your other options too.
- **Ask your employer for a payroll advance.** Many employers will advance wages you have *already earned*, interest-free. It never hurts to ask HR or your manager — this is more common than people assume.
- **Reach out to family or a friend.** A short, clearly agreed loan from someone you trust is often the cheapest bridge of all. Put the amount and repayment date in a quick text so everyone is on the same page.
- **Tap community and emergency programs.** If you are in genuine hardship, provincial emergency social assistance, local food banks, utility-relief programs and community agencies can cover essentials fast. Dialling **211** connects you to services in your area at no cost and with nothing to repay.

None of these carry interest, and most protect your credit and your peace of mind. They take a little more effort than tapping an app — but for many shortfalls, they are the smartest first call.

## What to Avoid When You Need Money Fast

Urgency is exactly when scammers and predatory lenders go hunting, because a stressed borrower is less likely to read the fine print. A few firm rules will keep you safe:

- **Be skeptical of "guaranteed approval, no credit check."** No legitimate lender can guarantee approval before reviewing anything. It is a marketing hook that often leads to sky-high fees — or an outright scam. Our guide on [how to spot predatory lenders](/resources/guides/how-to-spot-predatory-lenders) lists the tells.
- **Never pay a fee to *receive* a loan.** Real lenders deduct their costs from the loan or your payments — they do not ask you to e-transfer an "insurance," "processing" or "release" fee upfront. That advance-fee demand is one of the most common loan scams in Canada; see [avoiding loan scams](/resources/guides/avoiding-loan-scams).
- **Read the total cost, not the headline.** "0% interest" or "only $15" can still add up to a high effective rate once fees and short terms are included. Total the dollars.
- **Borrow only what you can repay on schedule.** The most expensive mistake is fixing today's shortfall with a loan that creates next month's. If money is tight *every* month, the answer is rarely a faster loan.

## The Bottom Line

Knowing how to get money fast in Canada is really about matching the tool to the situation. For a small, one-time gap, an interest-free advance from Bree, Nyble or KOHO Cover is quick and low-cost. For something larger, a same-day installment loan capped at 35% APR beats a payday loan every time you can repay over months instead of weeks. And whenever you can, the no-interest routes — selling an item, an instant-pay gig, a payroll advance, or community help — are worth trying first.

Above all, be kind to your future self. The fastest options are usually the priciest, so borrow only what you can comfortably pay back, and total every fee before you commit. If a shortfall keeps returning each month, the durable fix is not a faster loan — it is a small [emergency buffer](/resources/guides/emergency-fund-basics) and, if debt feels heavy, a conversation with a non-profit credit counselling agency.
